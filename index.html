Our Mission

Established in Montebello, this community library is a space for students of all ages to do school work, research new topics (using the library's databases), starting new projects, or reading in a cozy atmosphere. With our library having three floors, students are able to socialize and have a cup of coffee on the first floor. On the main floor we serve coffee and pastries. Here on the first floor you can access microwaves and we also rent out other supplies. The second floor is dedicated to studying or reading. The second floor offers multiple study rooms, conference rooms, and a computer lab ready to be reserved for class projects, club meetings, or zoom calls. The floor contains multiple printers and IT workers (university students) for any tech assistance. Lastly, the third floor is the quiet zone for students who prefer a quiet environment. We designed the building with the intention of helping people in Montebello to concentrate on their studies and encourage them to continue their education. We have also built with the intention to make it a safe space for many, whether it is to browse youtube videos in the computer lab, reading for fun, or listening to music (with headphones on please). This remodel was inspired by students from Montebello who noticed the importance of study spaces and accessibility for many students of the area.

Library Changes

To make the library more sustainable, we have planned to add more underground parking that will eliminate big pavements that are exposed to the sun. The rooftop will be covered in solar panels, where most of our energy will come from. For the parking lots which are currently being used, outside of the underground parking lot, we will add electric car changing stations.

As for materials and resources for making the second floor of the story, the majority of the building materials contain significant recycled content, such as the flooring (hardwood, carpet, tile), concrete, etc. We also plan to manufacture the building materials locally. Any new materials, such as wood, we plan to use from sustainable forests.

To enhance indoor environmental quality, smoking in the library will not be permitted or in the parking lot. To support safety and health of the people in the library, fresh air will be blended in the mechanical system. We will utilize natural sunlight, but on days of hotter climate, we will use a window covering system that will reduce heat gain. Paint, adhesive, and sealants we use will contain little to none volatile organic compounds.
